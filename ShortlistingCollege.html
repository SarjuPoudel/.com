Shortlisting Your College

Shortlisting colleges is an important step in your college search to make your time and effort worthwhile during the application process. After exploring through your several possible college choices, you should give each college a point based evaluation which indicates the best fitness of the colleges to your current and future needs. The process of shortlisting is heavily based on individual perspective and circumatances. However, giving a numeric value based on your preference makes it easier to decide how much time should your invest for each college applications.

Generally, you should consider major, cost, scholarship, application fee, essay questions, acceptance rate, SAT/TOEFL requirements, extracurricular activities, and career opportunities while shortlisting your colleges. It is suggested to make a college list that includes colleges in all of following categories based on their acceptance rate.

  • Safety Colleges:

    These colleges generally have a high acceptances rate and chances of getting "you" admitted is undeniable. Safety college may not not necessarily meet all your needs but fulfills your top priority i.e. Affordability. Depending on the academic history and SAT scores of students, the safety college list may differ for each students. These colleges do not take a lot of your time and effort to complete the application.

  • Mid-Safe Colleges:

    They are fairly competitive and meets all your expectations of college choice. The chances of getting you or a student with your academic performance is average. Your financial contribution may also affect your admittability in such college.

  • Top Notch College:

    These colleges are very competitive for students with your background. They may likely reject you based on your academic and apittude test scores. Despite low or no possibility of your admission, you should still apply to top notch college because these colleges looks overall aspect of your life rather than just academic performance. Your unique life experience may be just right fit for their college requirements. These college, in general, takes a lot of your time and effort.
