Applying for US VISA

Congratulation once again for your admission to the college of your choice. At this phase, you have applied, been accepted, and already decided which college you will attend. Now is your time to travel to US. Landing into the US ground requires you to have a VISA. A VISA is the permission issued to you to travel to US through air, land, or water ways.

For students, US immigration issues F1 and J1 visa depending on the nature of your program. F1 visa are issued to a degree seeking (i.e. High School/Associate/Bachelor/Masters/Phd) students while J1 are for vocational training and short term study programs.

VISA Application Process

  1. Get your I20 and Your Highest Educational Degree.
  2. Take Passport Size Photo for you VISA Application.
  3. Fill out the DS160 Form for your F1 VISA Application.
  4. Print out two passport size photo for the VISA interview.
  5. After you submit DS160, you are required to pay SEVIS Fee.
  6. Choose your VISA interview date and prepare for your visa.
  7. Take your passport, two copy of PP size photo, and academic documents
  8. Got your VISA? You will be asked to pay MRP service fee
  9. You will receive your passport in few days.

For more information, please visit official US Embassy Website.

Preparing for F1 VISA Questions

To qualify for US VISA, you should prove your intent to study, to have a temporary stay, to be able to afford college. VSIA interviewer will verify this information series of questions. In general, you should be prepared to answer following question.

  • Why you want to go to US?
  • How many colleges you applied to? Why your college?
  • Why are you taking this major?
  • What do you want to do after graduation?
  • How will you pay for your college?
  • Do you have relatives in USA?

Answer to your questions should be honest and plausible to visa interviewer to get the VISA.

Note: VISA is not a guarantee that you will be allowed to get in the country. Your entry is determined by CBP officer at the port of entry in the United States.
