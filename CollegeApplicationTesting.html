College Application: Standarized Tests

Standarized Tests are a part of the wholesome process of the College Application. These test allows you to show your academic potential in a way colleges in the US understand. Commonly taken standarized tests for undergraduate programs are SAT with/with out Essay, ACT, and SAT Subjects. Though some colleges may not require standarized tests and rely on your academic performance, it is always beneficial to take such test to expand your available college choice. As these tests are taken by incoming undergraduates all around the world, it offers college an evaluation reference to compare your academic performance with other students of similar background.

    Information on Standarized Tests

  • Scholastic Assessment Test (SAT): It is conducted by CollegeBoard and test your reasoning, writing, analytical, and problem solving skills. It consists of Reading, Writing, Math-No Calculator and Math-Calculator sections. It is quite popular and conducted in several test centers. Test center availability may be affected by the date of registration.
  • American College Testing (ACT): It is administered by ACT Inc. and covers English, Math, science and optional writing section. It has same purpose like SAT test but being less popular test centers availability may be limited. It can be taken as an alternative to SAT test. This test have additional science sections testing your knowledge of physics, biology, and chemistry.
  • SAT Subject Test: Just like SAT, it is organized by CollegeBoard. It is design to test your knowledge with several subjects like Physics, Maths, Biology, Chemistry, Language, and History. You can only take 3 SAT Subjects during one seating.
  • Test of English as a Foreign Language (TOEFL): Unlike the above standarized tests, this is just a language test. It test your ability to read, write, listen, and speak English language. It is internet based exam taken in test centers. It is prefered language test in most of the US colleges.
  • International English Language Testing System (IELTS): It is an another English language test that can be taken as alternative to TOEFL. Unlike TOEFL, it is preferred less by the US colleges, but accepted by colleges outside the US. You may take this test if you have an alternate plans to apply to other contries for higher education.

    • More information on testing, practise materials, and books are available from the official sources. Some third party sources may also provide similar testing materials and help you prepare for standarized tests.

    Testing Costs and its Minimization

    Cost of taking standarized tests is one of the major cost for college application. Due to unfamiliarity with testing materials and testing anxiety, you may not received an expected test scores. This may increase your testing cost for retaking tests. In addition, reporting test scores to colleges will add up your cost. However, there are few ways to reduce cost which are detailed below.

  • Use free scores reporting available during test registration.
  • Ask college if they would accept a PDF test score report (aka Unofficial Test Score Report) available to you from testing agency.
  • Check if your college would waive language test based on your high SAT/ACT scores for Reading and Writing sections.
  • If you took IELTS test, ask if college will accept it instead of TOEFL.
  • Check if your college would like to verify your test scores by accessing your testing portal.* Request only if you have a close connection to admission official.
  • If you are Opportunity Fund Participants of USEF-Nepal, please check with your advisor for any alternatives.
